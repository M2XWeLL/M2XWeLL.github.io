About AI

Artificial intelligence (AI) is rapidly transforming our world, and its impact on the labor market is a topic of intense discussion. This research report explores the multifaceted effects of AI on the workforce, delving into potential job displacement due to automation, the rise of new AI-driven roles, and the transformation of existing jobs. We will also examine the critical skills needed to thrive in the AI-powered workplace, the role of policy in mitigating disruptions and promoting adaptation, and the potential for AI to empower workers and improve work processes.

Impact on Jobs

The rise of AI presents both challenges and opportunities for the labor market. On the one hand, automation powered by AI threatens to disrupt sectors reliant on routine and repetitive tasks. Jobs in manufacturing, data entry, transportation, and customer service are often cited as being at high risk of automation. Studies by the McKinsey Global Institute (https://www.mckinsey.com/~/media/mckinsey/featured%20insights/Digital%20Disruption/Harnessing%20automation%20for%20a%20future%20that%20works/MGI-A-future-that-works-Executive-summary.ashx) estimate that up to 800 million jobs globally could be impacted by automation by 2030. The Oxford Martin Programme on the Future of Work (https://www.oxfordmartin.ox.ac.uk/future-of-work/) predicts that nearly half of all jobs could be automated by 2035. However, the picture is not entirely bleak. While some jobs may be lost to automation, AI is also creating entirely new job opportunities. The demand for data scientists, AI ethicists, cybersecurity specialists, and human-AI collaboration specialists is growing rapidly. These jobs require expertise in analyzing data, designing and implementing AI systems ethically, ensuring their security, and facilitating seamless collaboration between humans and AI.
